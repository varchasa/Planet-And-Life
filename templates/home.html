LIFE HABITABILITY

Mercury

Extreme temperatures both cold and hot make it unlikely that life may develop there. The temperatures and solar radiation that characterize this planet are most likely too extreme for organisms to adapt to.

Venus

Recently it has been discovered that Venus has a large vortex at both its poles. The altitude is estimated to be about 59 km which is just above the cloud deck and the air pressure and temperature are tolerable for Earth standards.

Earth

Life forms inhabit ecosystems, whose total is sometimes said to form a biosphere. The evolution of the biosphere is speculated to have begun about 3.5 Gya. The biosphere is divided into biomes, inhabited by broadly similar plants and animals.

Mars

In july 2018,scientists discovered sub-glacial lake on Mars, the first table body of water on the planet. Out of all the planets in the Solar System, Mars appears to have the highest change of having life forms but still the conditions are harsh enough that nothing should be able to survive there, perhaps only beneath the surface.

Jupiter

Since it doesn’t have a true surface but rather swirling fluids it is not conducive to life as we know it. Ganymede, Callisto, and Europa on the other hand, have higher chances of sustaining life.

Saturn

Since it doesn’t have a true surface, but rather swirling fluids, it is not conducive to life as we know it. The moons of Saturn however, respectively Titan and Enceladus, have internal oceans that could possibly hold life.

Uranus

Since it doesn’t have a true surface but rather swirling fluids it is not conducive to life as we know it.

Neptune

Since it doesn’t have a true surface but rather swirling fluids it is not conducive to life as we know it. Neptune’s moon Triton however, appears more likely to develop life.


INSIGHTS

  • Mars reaches opposition today, Oct. 13, offering skywatchers an early Halloween treat.
  • During opposition, Mars, Earth and the sun form a straight line, with Earth in the middle.
  • As a result, the Red Planet appears bigger, brighter and redder than usual — and the planet won't get this close to Earth again until 2035
  • Mars orbits the sun at a greater distance than Earth and takes about 26 months to complete one orbit around the sun.
  • For this year's Mars opposition, observers in the Northern Hemisphere will have an exceptional view of the Red Planet
  • Indeed, Mars won't be comparably close and well-positioned for northern observers again until it reaches opposition in 2052.

An AI just found 50 new planets

In recent development, an AI algorithm has discovered 50 new planets.

Here is all you need to read about that.

But this discovery marks a major push for the use of machine learning in the field of astronomy.


An algorithm was developed by NASA researchers at Warwick University that found some planets by examining throughly the data collected by Kepler space telescope. The observation from the last decade long mission had gathered information on thousands of potentional exoplanets by capturing a distinctive dip in the light from distant stars.

The researchers trained the algorithm on two large samples of confirmed planets and false positives from keplers data and get the planet-detection system ready.Using the information gathered, it finds actual batch of 50 new planets ranging size between neptune and earth.

With this rapid and automated tech, astronomers hope to validate thousands of potential exoplanets in the future.Taking investigation further through dedicated telescopes, in the search for potentially habitable worlds that could have conditions similar to those on earth.
